# Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** May 31, 2026  
**Data controller (public demo / operator-hosted instance):** Chaitanya Prabuddha  
**Contact:** https://github.com/chayprabs/pdf-visual-diff-online · https://www.chaitanyaprabuddha.com  
**Service:** PdfDiff

This policy explains how we handle information when you use PdfDiff. **Self-hosted** deployments are operated by third parties; their operators are separate controllers and may adopt different practices.

---

## 1. Scope

This policy applies to:

- The public website and playground  
- API requests to an instance we operate  
- Repository and security communications related to the project  

It does **not** apply to third-party websites linked from the app (GitHub, social profiles, etc.).

## 2. Summary

- We process PDFs **only to provide comparison results**.  
- Uploads and artifacts are **ephemeral** (default TTL about one hour).  
- We do **not** sell personal information or use uploads for advertising.  
- We do **not** intentionally log PDF content or passwords.  
- You are responsible for not uploading unlawful or unnecessary personal data.

## 3. Information we process

| Category | Examples | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Uploaded documents** | PDF files you submit | Generate diff, masks, summaries, bundles |
| **Passwords (optional)** | PDF decryption passwords | Open encrypted PDFs you provide |
| **Technical data** | IP address, user agent, timestamps, HTTP status, job IDs | Security, abuse prevention, operations |
| **Cookies / local storage** | Minimal session or preference data (if any) | Site functionality |
| **Communications** | GitHub issues, security reports | Support and security |

We do **not** require account registration for the public playground.

## 4. Legal bases (EEA, UK, Switzerland)

Where GDPR or UK GDPR applies, we rely on:

| Processing | Legal basis |
|------------|-------------|
| Providing the comparison service | **Contract** (Art. 6(1)(b)) or **steps at your request** before a contract |
| Security, abuse prevention, logs | **Legitimate interests** (Art. 6(1)(f)) — balanced against your rights |
| Compliance with law | **Legal obligation** (Art. 6(1)(c)) |
| Optional features requiring consent | **Consent** (Art. 6(1)(a)) where applicable |

You may object to legitimate-interest processing as described in Section 10.

## 5. Retention

| Data | Retention |
|------|-----------|
| Uploaded PDFs and generated artifacts | Deleted automatically after **TTL expiry** (default **1 hour**, configurable by operator) |
| Server logs | Typically **short** operational retention (e.g. days to weeks) unless longer retention is required for security or law |
| GitHub / email communications | Per GitHub or your email provider policies |

We do not retain PDF **content** in application logs by design.

## 6. Sharing and subprocessors

We do **not** sell or rent your personal information.

We may share data with:

- **Infrastructure providers** (hosting, CDN) that process data on our instructions to run the service  
- **Authorities** if required by valid legal process  
- **Advisers** under confidentiality where necessary to protect rights  

A public demo may run on providers such as cloud hosts in the **United States** or other countries. Where required, we rely on appropriate safeguards (e.g. Standard Contractual Clauses) for international transfers.

## 7. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures (ephemeral storage, access controls, TLS in transit, no intentional logging of file contents). **No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.** Use at your own risk for highly sensitive documents; consider **self-hosting** on infrastructure you control.

## 8. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to:

- **Access** personal data we hold about you  
- **Rectification** of inaccurate data  
- **Erasure** (“right to be forgotten”)  
- **Restriction** of processing  
- **Portability** (structured, machine-readable format where applicable)  
- **Object** to processing based on legitimate interests  
- **Withdraw consent** where processing is consent-based  
- **Lodge a complaint** with a supervisory authority  

**Ephemeral uploads:** Because files are deleted on TTL, erasure often occurs automatically; contact us if you believe data remains.

**How to exercise rights:** Open a GitHub issue on the repository (describe your request and jurisdiction) or contact via the website. We may verify identity where reasonable.

### 8.1 California (CCPA/CPRA)

California residents: we do **not** “sell” or “share” personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. Categories processed in the last 12 months may include identifiers, internet activity, and documents you upload. Rights include know, delete, correct, and non-discrimination. Authorized agents may submit requests with proof of authority.

### 8.2 Other U.S. states

Residents of states with privacy laws (Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, etc.) may have similar rights; contact us as above.

### 8.3 Brazil (LGPD), Canada (PIPEDA), Australia

You may have rights under local law; contact us to exercise them.

## 9. Children

The service is **not directed to children under 13** (or 16 in the EEA where applicable). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child provided data, contact us for deletion.

## 10. Automated processing

Diff results are produced by automated algorithms. We do **not** use uploads for automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects about individuals without human involvement.

## 11. Your responsibilities

- Do not upload personal data unless necessary; **minimize** sensitive content.  
- Ensure you have a lawful basis to process any personal data in PDFs (GDPR Art. 6, employment law, etc.).  
- Redact or avoid uploading special-category data (health, biometrics, etc.) unless you have explicit legal grounds and safeguards.

## 12. Self-hosted deployments

If you run PdfDiff yourself, **you** are the data controller for your users’ data. This policy does not govern your instance. Configure `ARTIFACT_TTL_SECONDS`, access controls, and your own privacy notice.

## 13. Changes

We may update this policy with a new “Last updated” date. Material changes may be highlighted on the site. Continued use after changes constitutes acknowledgment where permitted by law.

## 14. No contractual third-party beneficiaries

Except as required by law, this policy does not create enforceable rights in third parties.
